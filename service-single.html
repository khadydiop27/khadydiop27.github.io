    Learning how to read
    With anything in language learning, it takes practice! In order to read and write in Russian you must learn two different alphabets. In order to read, you must know the print alphabet and in order to write, you must learn the cursive alphabet. These alphabets are quite similar with few exceptions.     Subject Pronouns

    Subject Pronouns in Russian are very similar to those in English. The only exception is that Russian contains a formal and an informal "you". The informal "you" or "Вы"" is often used when you are talking to people whom you do not have an established relationship with or in formal settings.
    For example, at work, with your coworkers you would use the formal Вы however at at home with family and friends, you would use the inform "you: or "ты". Below is a list of subject pronouns and their English equivalents
    Я - I
    ты - You (informal)
    Он/Она - He/she
    Мы - We
    Вы - You (Formal)
    Они - They

    Verb Conjugation

    In the language of Russian there are two standard types of verb conjugation. They are known as conjugation 1 and conjugation 2. This section will cover what verb conjugation is and the two forms of verb conjugation

    Conjugation 1

    Subject Pronouns Читать-To read Гулять-To walk
    Я Читаю Гуляю
    Ты Читаешь Гуляешь
    Он/Она Читает Гуляет
    Мы Читаем Гуляем
    Вы Читаете Гуляете
    Они Читают Гуляют


    Conjugation 2

    Subject Pronouns Говорить-To read Смотреть-To walk
    Я Говорю Cмотрю
    Ты Говоришь Смотришь
    Он/Она Говорит Смотрит
    Мы Говорим Смотрим
    Вы Говорите Смотрите
    Они Говорят Смотрят

    Poetry

    Poetry is a very important part of Russian literature and culture. Memorization of poems by famous poets in Russia is often a part of a child's education. It is good to memorize some poems in Russian as it helps with reading, memorization, and cultural awareness.

    Я вас любил-Alexander Pushkin
    Я вас любил: любовь ещё, быть может,
    В душе моей угасла не совсем;
    Но пусть она вас больше не тревожит;
    Я не хочу печалить вас ничем.
    Я вас любил безмолвно, безнадежно,
    То робостью, то ревностью томим;
    Я вас любил так искренно, так нежно,
    Как дай вам Бог любимой быть другим.




    English
    I loved you. And, it might well be, this notion
    Is not extinguished in my soul just yet;
    But may it cease to bring on you commotion;
    I do not wish to make you feel upset.
    I loved you mutely, hopelessly, and dearly,
    With bashful, jealous suffering one can’t know;
    I loved you tenderly and so sincerely,
    May God grant that another love you so. Russian Performative Poetry

    Declension
    Declension is the act of changing a noun or adjective to show its function in a sentence. This concept, for English speakers can be quiet difficult to understand and implement while speaking Russian because we to not use declsion in our language. Declension and the cases that come along with it, can seem difficult at first, however with time and practice declining will come quiet naturally.


    In Russian the subject pronoun, adjective, noun, and demonstratives in the sentence decline to fit the gender and plurality in a sentence. In Russian there are 6 cases.
